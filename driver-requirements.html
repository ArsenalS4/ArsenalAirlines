DRIVER CERTIFICATION

VEHICLE LICENSING & TRACK REQUIREMENTS

OVERVIEW

The WARDOGS Driver Certification program validates ground-vehicle operators across three specialized tracks. Each track has a written exam and a practical evaluation; passing the written exam (90% minimum) is required to schedule an in-game practical check ride with an instructor.

Tip: Choose the track that matches the vehicles and tactical role you intend to operate in-game.

TRACK 1: SMALL TRANSPORT

Focus: Fast, agile light vehicles used for rapid insertion, hit-and-run, and tactical off-road navigation.

  • Vehicle handling at high speed and low traction
  • Tactical off-road route selection and obstacle negotiation
  • Speed-run techniques and safe high-speed cornering
  • Quick-exfil and immediate threat reaction

EXAM FOCUS: vehicle dynamics, traction management, evasive driving, and terrain assessment.

TRACK 2: HEAVY TRANSPORT

Focus: Large logistics vehicles and multi-passenger transports with emphasis on payload management and safety.

  • Payload weight distribution and center-of-gravity effects
  • Climb/descent techniques on steep inclines and rough gradients
  • Braking limits under load and emergency stopping procedures
  • Convoy coordination, vehicle spacing, and casualty extraction procedures

EXAM FOCUS: load calculations, engine/brake management, convoy tactics, and vehicle survivability.

TRACK 3: WEAPONIZED

Focus: Armored and weaponized platforms — APCs, IFVs, tanks, and self-propelled artillery operations.

  • Operating heavy armor: mobility, concealment, and survivability
  • Maneuvering with mounted weapons and coordinating fire support
  • Armor angling, sloped-armor tactics, and reducing penetration from AP rounds
  • Communication with gunners and combined-arms movement discipline

EXAM FOCUS: vehicle armor mechanics, angling strategies, weapon integration, and team coordination.

PRACTICAL EVALUATION & RULES

COMMON RULES OF ENGAGEMENT:

  • CRASH = FAIL - Major vehicle destruction or catastrophic contact during evaluation results in immediate failure.
  • POOR TACTICS = FAIL - Failure to maintain convoy discipline, expose dismounts, or misuse of vehicle assets is grounds for fail.
  • Precision in parking/exfil maneuvers and timely execution of orders are assessed.
  • Demonstrate proper vehicle maintenance checks and threat-assessment prior to movement.

After passing the written exam for a chosen track, complete the post-exam certification form to transmit your results and schedule an in-game practical with an instructor.
